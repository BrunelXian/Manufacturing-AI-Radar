# Digital Twin

## What This Domain Covers

This domain covers digital twin concepts and systems for manufacturing, where virtual representations of assets, processes, or production systems remain linked to physical operation through data and models.

## Why It Matters In Manufacturing

Digital twin systems provide a system-level framework for combining physical models, process data, machine learning, and operational decision-making. They are important because they can turn isolated AI models into integrated manufacturing intelligence infrastructure.

## Typical Tasks

- virtual process representation
- state synchronization between physical and digital systems
- what-if simulation
- predictive quality analysis
- system-level optimisation support
- anomaly interpretation and diagnosis

## Representative Methods

- physics-based simulation
- hybrid physics-ML modelling
- data assimilation
- state estimation and synchronization
- machine learning-enhanced surrogate simulation
- optimisation over virtual process replicas

## Example Application Scenarios

- additive manufacturing twins for process tracking and quality prediction
- machine health twins for maintenance planning
- production line twins for throughput and disturbance analysis
- operator-facing twin systems for decision support
- simulation-backed optimisation of manufacturing settings

## Key Research Questions

- what makes a digital twin genuinely operational rather than only descriptive
- how should data fidelity and model fidelity be balanced
- how can twins remain synchronized in noisy industrial environments
- where does AI add the most value within twin architectures
- how can twin systems support trust and usability for engineers and operators

## Future Opportunity

The biggest opportunity lies in making digital twins actionable, lightweight, and connected to real decision workflows rather than treating them as standalone visualization artifacts.
