# Process Modelling

## What This Domain Covers

This domain covers AI and hybrid methods that represent manufacturing process behavior, state transitions, and process-to-quality relationships.

The emphasis is on models that help explain, approximate, or simulate manufacturing dynamics in ways that are useful for prediction, optimisation, and control.

## Why It Matters In Manufacturing

Manufacturing systems are often expensive to experiment on and difficult to describe perfectly with first-principles models alone. Process models provide a practical middle ground between physical insight and operational utility.

## Typical Tasks

- surrogate modelling
- process-to-quality mapping
- state transition modelling
- uncertainty-aware response modelling
- hybrid physics-data representation

## Representative Methods

- regression and ensemble learning
- Gaussian processes
- neural surrogates
- physics-informed machine learning
- reduced-order hybrid models
- sequence models for dynamic process behavior

## Example Application Scenarios

- approximating melt pool response in additive manufacturing
- modelling residual stress trends
- estimating microstructure evolution under process conditions
- representing machine dynamics for downstream control design
- building fast simulation proxies for parameter exploration

## Key Research Questions

- when is a purely data-driven model sufficient
- how should physical constraints be incorporated into learning systems
- how can uncertainty be estimated in a deployment-relevant way
- how transferable are models across materials, geometries, and machines
- what level of model fidelity is needed for control and optimisation

## Future Opportunity

The strongest future opportunity is in hybrid manufacturing models that are both computationally efficient and physically credible.
