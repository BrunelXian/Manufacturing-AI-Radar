# Closed-loop Control

## What This Domain Covers

This domain covers AI-assisted control strategies that use process observations or predicted states to adjust manufacturing actions during operation.

It includes adaptive control, learning-enhanced control, and closed-loop quality regulation in manufacturing processes.

## Why It Matters In Manufacturing

Monitoring and prediction become much more valuable when they can influence the process before quality is lost. Closed-loop control is one of the most important transition points between information and action in manufacturing AI.

## Typical Tasks

- online parameter adjustment
- disturbance compensation
- trajectory correction
- quality-consistent regulation
- adaptive set-point selection
- controller tuning with learned process information

## Representative Methods

- adaptive control
- model predictive control
- observer-based control with learned state estimation
- data-driven controller tuning
- reinforcement learning for sequential control policies
- hybrid rule-based and learned decision systems

## Example Application Scenarios

- laser power adaptation in additive manufacturing
- deposition rate correction in directed energy deposition
- welding parameter control from weld pool observations
- feed rate and tool path adjustments in machining
- robotic manufacturing systems with online path correction

## Key Research Questions

- how can learned controllers remain stable and safe
- what sensing latency can control systems tolerate
- how should uncertainty be handled in feedback decisions
- when is model predictive control preferable to reinforcement learning
- how should AI control strategies be validated for industrial adoption

## Future Opportunity

A major future opportunity lies in deployment-ready closed-loop systems that combine interpretable monitoring, uncertainty-aware prediction, and constrained control action selection.
