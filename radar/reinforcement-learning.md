# Reinforcement Learning

## What This Domain Covers

This domain covers reinforcement learning approaches for manufacturing problems that require sequential decision-making under uncertainty.

The focus includes process control, robotic operations, production decisions, and adaptive strategies that are difficult to specify with fixed rules alone.

## Why It Matters In Manufacturing

Many manufacturing decisions are sequential, partially observed, and sensitive to delayed consequences. Reinforcement learning is attractive because it can learn policies from interaction, optimize long-term reward, and adapt to changing conditions.

## Typical Tasks

- adaptive process control
- robotic manipulation and assembly
- dynamic scheduling
- policy learning under uncertainty
- multi-step optimisation with delayed quality outcomes

## Representative Methods

- Q-learning and deep value-based methods
- policy gradient methods
- actor-critic methods
- model-based reinforcement learning
- offline reinforcement learning
- safe reinforcement learning

## Example Application Scenarios

- adaptive control policies for additive manufacturing
- robotic handling in flexible manufacturing cells
- manufacturing scheduling with changing disturbances
- sequential parameter adjustment during quality-sensitive production
- simulation-to-real policy transfer for industrial robots

## Key Research Questions

- how can reinforcement learning be made data-efficient enough for manufacturing
- how should safety constraints be embedded into policy learning
- how realistic are current simulation environments
- when is offline reinforcement learning a better path than online interaction
- how should learned policies be validated before industrial deployment

## Future Opportunity

The most promising opportunity is reinforcement learning integrated with process models, simulators, constraints, and operator oversight.
