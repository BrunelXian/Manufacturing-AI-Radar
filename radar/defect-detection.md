# Defect Detection

## What This Domain Covers

This domain covers AI methods used to identify, localize, classify, or predict manufacturing defects.

It includes both in-process defect indication and post-process inspection, spanning visible surface issues as well as hidden quality risks inferred from process data or indirect measurements.

## Why It Matters In Manufacturing

Defects are a direct interface between manufacturing intelligence and production value. Defect detection affects scrap reduction, certification workflows, rework cost, throughput, and trust in advanced manufacturing processes.

## Typical Tasks

- defect classification
- defect localization
- image segmentation
- anomaly screening
- defect risk estimation
- post-process inspection support

## Representative Methods

- convolutional neural networks for inspection imagery
- segmentation models for defect localization
- anomaly detection for rare or poorly labeled defects
- multimodal defect inference from process and inspection data
- self-supervised representation learning where labels are limited

## Example Application Scenarios

- pore and lack-of-fusion indication in additive manufacturing
- crack detection in weld seams
- surface quality inspection for machined or formed parts
- defect screening in industrial imaging systems
- process-signal-based early warning of downstream quality problems

## Key Research Questions

- how can rare defects be learned under limited labels
- how can models distinguish between true defects and benign process variation
- how early can defects be predicted before final inspection
- which forms of explainability are useful for industrial quality teams
- how should inspection models be validated for high-consequence manufacturing use

## Future Opportunity

The most valuable future direction is linking monitoring, defect inference, and root-cause reasoning so that AI supports not only detection, but also prevention.
