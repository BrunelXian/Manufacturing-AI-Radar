# Additive Manufacturing

## What This Domain Covers

This domain covers AI methods applied to additive manufacturing processes, especially metal and high-value industrial systems such as `LPBF`, `DED`, `LDED`, and `WAAM`.

The focus is on how AI supports process understanding, monitoring, quality prediction, parameter selection, defect prevention, and adaptive control across layer-wise fabrication workflows.

## Why It Matters In Manufacturing

Additive manufacturing is one of the richest environments for manufacturing AI because the process is data-intensive, highly dynamic, and sensitive to parameter variation. Small disturbances can affect melt pool behavior, geometry, porosity, residual stress, and final part performance.

## Typical Tasks

- in-situ process monitoring
- build quality prediction
- melt pool state estimation
- defect risk prediction
- parameter optimisation
- process stability assessment
- closed-loop process adjustment

## Representative Methods

- computer vision for melt pool and plume observation
- multimodal learning from thermal, optical, acoustic, and machine signals
- surrogate models for process-property relationships
- physics-informed learning for thermal and material behavior
- anomaly detection for build irregularities
- reinforcement learning and adaptive control for parameter updates

## Example Application Scenarios

- monitoring melt pool morphology during `LPBF` to infer process stability
- predicting porosity risk from in-situ imaging and machine parameters
- estimating residual stress trends from process histories
- optimizing laser power and scan speed combinations for target quality windows
- adapting deposition parameters in directed energy deposition systems

## Key Research Questions

- how reliably can in-situ data predict final part quality
- how should process physics and machine learning be combined
- what level of sensing is necessary for practical deployment
- how transferable are models across machines, materials, and geometries
- how can control policies remain safe in high-value manufacturing environments

## Future Opportunity

The biggest opportunity is to connect process understanding, quality prediction, and adaptive control into a unified manufacturing intelligence loop.
