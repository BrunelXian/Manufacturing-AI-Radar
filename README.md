Manufacturing-AI-Radar

A structured technology radar for Artificial Intelligence in Manufacturing.

This repository aims to map, organise, and interpret the rapidly growing landscape of AI-driven manufacturing technologies, with a focus on industrial applications such as additive manufacturing, process monitoring, intelligent control, and digital twins.

Unlike typical "awesome lists" that simply collect papers or repositories, Manufacturing-AI-Radar focuses on building a structured knowledge map of the field — highlighting how different AI techniques are actually used across manufacturing processes.

Why This Repository Exists

Artificial Intelligence has become one of the most influential technological forces in modern engineering.

However, most AI resources today focus on:

Large Language Models
General Machine Learning
Software engineering applications

In contrast, AI applications in manufacturing are fragmented and poorly organised across different disciplines such as:

mechanical engineering
materials science
robotics
control systems
industrial AI

As a result, it is difficult for researchers and engineers to understand:

what problems AI is solving in manufacturing
which techniques are actually used
where the real research opportunities lie

Manufacturing-AI-Radar attempts to provide a structured view of this emerging field.

Scope

The radar focuses on AI methods applied to manufacturing processes, including but not limited to:

Additive Manufacturing (LPBF, LDED, WAAM)
Welding processes
Machining
Forming
Smart manufacturing systems

The goal is not to cover all AI research, but specifically AI that interacts with physical manufacturing processes.

Core Radar Domains

The repository organises the field into several key domains.

1. Sensing & Monitoring

Understanding the manufacturing process through sensor data.

Examples include:

melt pool monitoring
thermal imaging
acoustic sensing
optical monitoring
multi-modal sensing systems

Typical AI methods:

computer vision
signal processing
anomaly detection
2. Defect Detection

AI methods used to identify defects during or after manufacturing.

Examples:

porosity detection in additive manufacturing
crack detection
surface defect inspection

Typical approaches:

convolutional neural networks
segmentation models
anomaly detection
3. Process Modelling

Machine learning models used to predict or approximate manufacturing processes.

Examples:

surrogate models
physics-informed machine learning
hybrid physics-ML models

Typical tasks:

predicting melt pool behaviour
predicting residual stress
predicting microstructure evolution
4. Intelligent Control

Using AI to adjust process parameters during manufacturing.

Examples:

adaptive process control
AI-assisted parameter tuning
closed-loop manufacturing systems

Typical methods:

reinforcement learning
adaptive optimisation
model predictive control with ML
5. Reinforcement Learning in Manufacturing

Reinforcement learning is increasingly used for decision-making in complex manufacturing processes.

Typical applications include:

adaptive control of additive manufacturing
robotic manipulation
process optimisation under uncertainty

These systems often model manufacturing processes as partially observable decision problems.

6. Digital Twins

Digital representations of manufacturing systems that integrate:

physical models
sensor data
machine learning

Digital twins enable:

predictive manufacturing
process simulation
AI-assisted optimisation
